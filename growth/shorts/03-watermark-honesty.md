# Short Video 03 — "Why the preview has a watermark"

**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Length:** 30 seconds (short + punchy)
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical
**Shooting time:** ~15 min. Editing: ~30 min.

## Concept

Addresses the #1 objection head-on: "is this a scam?" Turns the
watermarked preview from a "limitation" into the single most trust-building
feature of the product. This video is pure conversion armor.

**Earle Coates** — strong face, mid-century, emotionally readable.

## Edit notes

- Key visual: the watermarked image MUST read on screen at 9:16. If the
  watermark tile is too dense it looks like glitch art. Might need to
  regenerate the preview at a higher resolution just for this shot
  (use the actual site output, not a mockup).
- The side-by-side at 5–9s is the money shot. $6.90 price overlay should
  be huge, readable in half a second.
- At 13–18s, 18–22s, 22–26s — each reason should have its OWN color
  accent (e.g., warm gold for "you never pay blind") to let viewers
  visually bucket the three points.

## Post-production checklist

- [ ] Watermark visible but not overwhelming in 0–9s shot
- [ ] Price overlay ($6.90) animates in, not just appears
- [ ] Each numbered reason uses the same font size and animates in
      the same way (consistency signals "this is a list, trust it")
- [ ] Site URL is the last thing on screen, held for 3+ seconds
