# Short Video 05 — "Restoring a photo in real time (one take)"

**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Length:** 55 seconds
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical
**Shooting time:** ~15 min (single take). Editing: ~30 min.

## Concept

Real-time product demo with no cuts. Phone screen-recording the entire
artgen.site/ai-hd flow from empty state to watermarked preview in one
continuous clip. Powerful because it proves the tool is real, fast, and
has no hidden step. This video converts the skeptical "yeah but what does
it actually look like to use it" viewer.

## Photo to use

Your own phone camera roll — pick any old family photo you actually have
(NOT one of the archive examples — viewers can tell). If you don't have
one, use the **Carver 1910** before image as a proxy.

If proxy: `artgen.site/examples/old-photos/pair-carver-1910-before.jpg`
→ save it to your phone first, then use it as the upload source.

## Shooting instructions

1. Open the iOS Screen Recorder (Control Center → big red dot).
2. Open Safari → `artgen.site/ai-hd`.
3. Before you hit record: have the damaged photo already in your Photos.
4. Start recording.
5. Scroll so the "Upload your photo" card is dead center.
6. Tap "Choose a photo," pick the damaged image.
7. Tap "I agree" checkbox.
8. Tap "Generate AI preview (free)."
9. **Don't stop recording** — let the viewer see the ~60-second processing
   screen. This is WHY the demo works. They see the wait isn't fake.
10. When the watermarked preview appears, scroll through it slowly so the
    viewer sees the quality.
11. Stop recording.

You'll have a ~90s clip. In editing, speed up the "processing" section to
10x so the whole video fits in 55s, but DO NOT cut it — a jump cut during
processing destroys the credibility this video is built on.

## Edit notes

- DO NOT cut during the processing segment. Speed up only. Cuts here
  are perceived as "the developer is hiding something."
- Leave the iOS status bar visible (time, battery, signal) at the top
  — it's subtle proof this is a real phone, not a screen mockup.
- The moment the watermarked preview first appears is the climax. Let
  it hold for 2 full seconds before the voiceover continues.
- The scroll through the preview at 35–45s should be slow and
  deliberate. Viewers are making a quality judgment right now.
