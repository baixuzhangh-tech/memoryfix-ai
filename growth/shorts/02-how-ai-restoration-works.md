# Short Video 02 — "How this AI decides what to keep"

**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
**Length:** 45 seconds
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical
**Shooting time:** ~25 min. Editing: ~45 min.

## Concept

Educational / trust-building. Uses the Worthington 1910 photo. Shows
viewers what a GOOD AI restoration is supposed to PRESERVE rather than
change, so they can trust the tool. This video is the "we're not going
to turn grandma into a stranger" message.

## Photo to use

**Worthington 1910** — family group portrait. Has multiple faces, clear
period clothing, background backdrop — perfect for demonstrating the
"preservation" message.

- Before: `artgen.site/examples/old-photos/old-family-worthington-1910-B.png`
- After: `artgen.site/examples/new-photos/old-family-worthington-1910-A.png`

## Edit notes

- The red-circle overlays are critical — they are the visual proof of
  the "it doesn't change the face" claim. If you can't do motion graphics,
  use Canva or Kapwing — drop a red ellipse on each face, animate in with
  0.2s fade.
- The CONTRAST ↑ / FACES = list on screen 14–20s is the "receipt" of the
  whole video. Make it land slowly, one item at a time, with a soft SFX tick
  on each.
- Cover image: the zoomed-in face AFTER (14–20s frame) with "SAME EYES.
  SAME PERSON." as caption
